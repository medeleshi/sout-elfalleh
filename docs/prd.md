## Document Information
- **Project Name:** Sout El Falah
- **Product Type:** Web Application
- **Version:** V7
- **Document Type:** Product Requirements Document
- **Target Market:** Tunisia
- **Primary Audience:** Small and medium farmers
- **Language Strategy:** Arabic-first, with simple terminology suitable for Tunisian users

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Sout El Falah is a farmer-first digital platform designed for Tunisia. It combines community, marketplace, and messaging in one web application tailored to the daily realities of agricultural users.

The platform is designed not only to help farmers publish, sell, ask, and connect, but to create a daily high-value product experience that adapts to each user’s role, region, and intent.

Sout El Falah will serve its MVP through one shared product structure with personalized content, opportunities, and actions for farmers and buyers. The product should feel immediately useful for farmers and buyers in MVP, while remaining extensible for future monetized professional offerings without becoming complex or fragmented.

Rather than functioning as a generic social network or a static classifieds website, the platform should become a trusted, habit-forming digital environment for agricultural interaction in Tunisia.

The MVP will focus on the highest-value workflows:
- Farmers publishing listings
- Farmers publishing posts and questions
- Buyers discovering listings and contacting sellers
- Buyers publishing purchase requests
- Direct messaging between users
- Profile and trust-building
- Basic personalization across Home, Marketplace, and Notifications

Over time, Sout El Falah should evolve from a useful agricultural product into a highly relevant digital ecosystem that supports discovery, trade, communication, and continuous engagement every day.

## 3. Problem Statement

Farmers in Tunisia face several recurring problems:
- limited direct access to buyers,
- dependence on intermediaries,
- lack of fast and trusted agricultural advice,
- weak visibility for what is being bought and sold locally,
- poor digital experiences that are not adapted to farmers,
- and low discovery quality in existing platforms.

Existing platforms are often too generic, too urban, too complicated, or not centered around agricultural workflows.

Even when digital tools exist, they typically fail to show users the most relevant content, opportunities, and actions for their specific role and local context.

There is an opportunity to build a platform specifically for the Tunisian farmer, with simple language, localized content, and workflows built around real daily needs, while using personalization to make each session more useful, more relevant, and more engaging.

## 8. Core Product Principles

1. **Farmer-first, but multi-user ready**  
   The product is designed primarily for farmers, while still delivering strong value to buyers and traders in MVP.

2. **Simple language, high clarity**  
   The platform must use clear, direct, localized language that feels familiar and practical for Tunisian users.

3. **Action before browsing**  
   Every core screen should help the user do something useful quickly: sell, discover, ask, respond, contact, or manage.

4. **Shared structure, deeply personalized experience**  
   All users should recognize the same product structure, but the content, priorities, recommendations, and calls-to-action should adapt to who the user is and what they need.

5. **Relevance over volume**  
   The platform should not show more content just to fill space. It should show the most useful content, opportunities, and notifications for each user.

6. **Trust before growth**  
   Strong identity, ownership, transparency, moderation, and clear communication matter more than aggressive engagement tactics.

7. **Continuous engagement without overload**  
   The experience should keep users returning through meaningful relevance, not through spammy notifications or excessive advertising.

8. **Personalization with user control**  
   The system should personalize content and opportunities based on user data and behavior, while allowing users to influence what they see through preferences, filters, and notification settings.

## 9. User Roles and Personalization Strategy

### Shared Product Structure
All users will see the same core navigation and main product architecture:
- Home
- Marketplace
- Messages
- Notifications
- Profile
- Search
- Settings

This creates consistency, reduces learning effort, and keeps the product easy to navigate.

### Personalization Objective
The goal of personalization is to make the platform feel immediately useful for every user type by showing:
- more relevant content,
- more relevant opportunities,
- more relevant people,
- and more relevant actions.

Personalization should improve:
- daily engagement,
- discovery quality,
- conversion into messaging,
- listing and post interaction,
- and repeat usage.

### Personalization Inputs
The platform will personalize the experience using a combination of:

#### Explicit Signals
- user role
- activity type
- crop or livestock focus
- governorate / region
- language preference
- profile information
- stated interests

#### Behavioral Signals
- viewed posts
- viewed listings
- searches performed
- categories browsed
- messages initiated
- content created
- repeated interactions
- saved, hidden, or reported items

#### Contextual Signals
- recency
- regional relevance
- seasonal relevance
- content type
- trust level of publisher
- similarity to previous user activity

### Personalization Layers

#### 1. Role-Based Personalization
Different user roles should see different default priorities.

- **Farmers** should see practical content, buyer demand, local opportunities, and problem-solving community posts.
- **Buyers** should see available listings, supply opportunities, seller activity, and matching products by location and category.

#### 2. Region-Based Personalization
Content should be more visible when it is geographically relevant to the user’s governorate, nearby production area, or operating zone.

#### 3. Intent-Based Personalization
The product should detect whether a user is primarily trying to:
- sell,
- buy,
- ask,
- explore,
- or communicate,

and adapt feed priorities and CTAs accordingly.

#### 4. Behavior-Based Personalization
Over time, the system should adapt to what the user actually interacts with, not only what they selected during onboarding.

### Dynamic Primary CTA by Role
- Farmer: **Add Listing** / **Ask a Question**
- Buyer: **Add Purchase Request**

### Personalization States
The platform should support multiple personalization maturity states:
- **Cold Start User** — personalization based on onboarding inputs, role, and region
- **Light Activity User** — personalization based on profile + early behavior
- **Established User** — personalization based on profile + behavior + interaction history
- **Low Content Availability User** — fallback to nearby, trending, editor-prioritized, or high-trust content

### Personalization Guardrails
To maintain quality, personalization must follow these rules:
- avoid repetitive content
- avoid over-prioritizing ads
- avoid surfacing low-trust or heavily reported content
- ensure diversity of content types
- balance recency with relevance
- prevent notification overload
- allow users to tune preferences where appropriate
- explain or signal relevance when helpful
- degrade stale or ignored content over time
- preserve room for organic community content alongside commercial content

### Retention and Feedback Loops
The product should create durable repeat usage through explicit loops, not only passive browsing.

#### Core Retention Loops
- publish listing -> receive views, saves, messages, and reminders -> return to manage or refresh listing
- publish question -> receive comments or direct messages -> return to review and continue discussion
- publish purchase request -> receive matching farmer responses -> return to compare and contact
- save listing, request, or post -> receive updates when status changes or matching content appears -> return to act
- follow seller, buyer, category, or location -> receive relevant new activity -> return to discover

#### Feedback Loops
Users should be able to improve personalization through lightweight feedback such as:
- save
- follow
- hide
- not interested
- show less like this
- report

These actions should affect future feed ranking, marketplace recommendations, and notification relevance.

## 11. Main Pages and Functional Requirements

### 11.1 Home
#### Goal
Serve as the user’s daily personalized landing page, helping them quickly discover the most relevant content, opportunities, and next actions.

#### Experience Principle
Home should feel useful within seconds. It should answer:
- What should I look at now?
- What should I do next?
- What opportunities are relevant to me today?

#### Key Sections
- Personalized welcome header
- Dynamic primary CTA
- Quick action panel
- Feed filters
- Main personalized feed
- Recommendation modules
- Right-side widgets (prices, weather, alerts, relevant opportunities)
- Suggested people, buyers, or sellers
- Personalized reminders or follow-ups

#### Feed Composition
The Home feed should include a balanced mix of:
- community posts
- agricultural questions
- listings
- purchase requests
- local alerts
- relevant opportunities
- selected promotional or sponsored items

Default composition should follow clear quotas to protect experience quality:
- majority organic content from community and marketplace activity
- strong representation of nearby or role-relevant opportunities
- recommendation modules inserted sparingly
- sponsored content kept to a clear minority of impressions

The system should not over-index on one content type unless user behavior strongly indicates that preference.

#### Feed Ranking Logic
The ranking of items in the feed should consider:
- user role relevance
- region relevance
- category relevance
- behavioral relevance
- recency
- publisher trust
- engagement quality
- content diversity

#### Functional Requirements
- Users can view a personalized feed
- Users can filter feed content by type
- Users can interact with posts and listings directly from the feed
- Users can click dynamic primary CTAs from the top of the page
- Users can hide or dismiss irrelevant content
- Users can save listings, posts, and requests
- Users can follow sellers, buyers, categories, and locations from feed surfaces
- The system can prioritize nearby and role-relevant opportunities
- The system can insert recommendation modules between feed items
- The system can reduce duplicate, repetitive, or low-value content
- The system can surface reminders tied to user activity, such as stale listings, unanswered questions, or new matches

#### Home Personalization by Role
For farmers:
- buyer demand
- practical questions and answers
- nearby listings and opportunities
- local alerts
- relevant agricultural content

For buyers:
- available listings
- matching sellers
- supply opportunities

- high-trust regional content

## 14. First Success and Liquidity Requirements

### First Success Principle
The product must help users achieve an early concrete win, not just complete setup. A first success may be:
- first relevant message received
- first reply to a question
- first save on a listing or request
- first useful match discovered

### First Success Requirements
- first-time sellers should receive structured guidance toward a complete listing
- first-time buyers should receive structured guidance toward a clear purchase request
- when a new listing or request receives no engagement, the system should trigger improvement prompts and broader fallback distribution where appropriate
- the system should help new users reach a first message as early as possible
- onboarding and Home should reinforce the next best action that leads to a real outcome, not only profile completion

### Liquidity and Market Density Requirements
The marketplace experience depends on enough supply and demand density to create useful matches. For MVP planning and launch, the product should assume:
- one to three priority launch regions, rather than nationwide breadth from day one
- a focused set of priority crops, livestock categories, or commercial segments
- explicit minimum content density thresholds for healthy discovery by category and region
- fallback behavior when a category or geography is too thin
- operational seeding plans for both supply and demand in priority areas

### Initial Intervention Defaults
For MVP, the system should apply simple default intervention logic before advanced automation exists:
- if a new listing gets no meaningful views within the first 24 to 48 hours, prompt the seller to improve title, image, location, or category and widen nearby distribution when appropriate
- if a listing gets views but no messages after an initial review window, prompt the seller to improve price clarity, quantity, images, or description
- if a purchase request gets no replies within the first 24 to 48 hours, prompt the buyer to improve specificity or broaden location and timing
- if a question gets no answer within an initial review window, re-surface it to relevant nearby users or category-relevant participants
- if a new user has not reached a first meaningful interaction within their first active session window, Home and notifications should emphasize the single next-best action most likely to create that outcome

### Launch Operating Strategy
The MVP launch should be operationally narrow and measurable:
- prioritize one to three launch geographies where supply and demand can both be seeded
- prioritize categories with repeat commercial need and visible local activity
- define minimum viable density per priority category-region pair before broad promotion
- review category-region liquidity weekly during launch
- reduce expansion speed if local match quality falls below acceptable thresholds
- measure launch readiness using useful matches and conversations, not only registrations
- treat registrations without discovery or messaging activity as insufficient proof of launch success

### Low-Liquidity Safeguards
- when exact local supply is low, the system should broaden to nearby areas before showing weak results
- when buyer demand is weak, the system should highlight adjacent opportunities, related categories, or encourage request creation
- when seller supply is weak, the system should encourage buyer requests and editorially boosted starter supply
- launch readiness should be evaluated by category-region liquidity, not only total registered users

The content system should make the platform feel alive, useful, and commercially active, while keeping trust and relevance high.

### Content Distribution Principle
The platform should maintain a healthy balance between:
- community knowledge
- market opportunities
- practical updates
- personalized recommendations
- selected commercial visibility

### Feed Priorities by Role
For farmers:
- practical posts
- buyer demand
- nearby opportunities
- problem-solving content
- relevant alerts
- market visibility

For buyers:
- available listings
- trusted sellers
- supply opportunities
- matching categories
- regional availability


### Sponsored Content Policy
Sponsored or promoted content may be included, but only when:
- it matches user relevance
- it does not interrupt task flow too aggressively
- it remains a minority of the content mix
- it supports user intent rather than distracting from it
- it stays within feed and marketplace quota rules that preserve organic trust

### Content Quality Rules
The system should favor:
- recent content
- useful content
- trusted publishers
- geographically relevant content
- diverse content mix
- complete listings and requests with strong metadata

The system should reduce visibility for:
- repeated content
- low-trust content
- irrelevant content
- heavily reported or low-quality items
- stale listings and outdated requests that were not refreshed

### Freshness and Lifecycle Rules
- Listings and requests should have freshness states such as fresh, aging, stale, and closed
- The system should prompt users to refresh stale items before continuing to distribute them widely
- Closed, sold, or expired items should stop receiving discovery exposure except in clearly archived states
- If an item is getting views but no contact, the system may prompt the owner to improve title, price, quantity, image, or description

## 17. Moderation and Trust

### MVP Trust Features
- visible user identity
- profile information
- ownership of posts and listings
- report content modal
- report user from chat and profile
- block user
- mute user
- delete confirmation
- basic identity confidence layer such as phone verification or equivalent account confidence signal
- basic moderation admin tooling later
- reduced visibility for low-quality and repeatedly reported content
- profile completeness progress
- recent activity visibility
- basic responsiveness indicators where appropriate
- basic anti-spam and suspicious behavior protection for posting and messaging

### Identity Confidence Defaults
For MVP, the trust model should be understandable and lightweight:
- accounts may exist in unverified or confidence-confirmed states
- confidence-confirmed users should receive clearer trust signaling in profile and messaging surfaces
- unverified users may browse, publish, and message, but repeated outbound outreach, suspicious posting volume, or abusive behavior should trigger stricter limits faster than for confidence-confirmed users
- confidence confirmation should be strongly encouraged before high-intent commercial activity such as repeated seller outreach or high posting frequency
- the product should preserve a path to publish quickly while encouraging confidence confirmation early
- ranking and recommendation systems should prefer confidence-confirmed publishers when quality is otherwise similar

### Future Trust Features
- verification badges
- ratings and reviews
- transaction reliability signals
- trust score inputs for ranking and recommendation quality

### Trust Principles
- direct communication should feel safe and clear
- ownership should always be visible
- promotional or sponsored visibility should not undermine credibility
- moderation should protect the usefulness of the feed and marketplace
- commercial trust should improve with real activity, responsiveness, and identity confidence

## 22. Required MVP Product Rules Before Final Design

### Retention Mechanics
The product should not rely only on passive discovery. The design and engineering teams must implement repeat-use loops through:
- saves
- follows
- activity-driven reminders
- freshness prompts
- direct response notifications
- return paths from notifications into the originating context

### Feed and Ad Quotas
For MVP, default quota rules should be:
- organic content should remain the clear majority of Home and Marketplace impressions
- sponsored content should remain a clear minority and never displace the strongest high-intent organic matches
- repeated exposure from the same publisher should be limited within a short session window
- Home should preserve a balance between community, market, and recommendation modules rather than collapsing into a single content type
- recommendation modules should be inserted sparingly and should not interrupt consecutive high-intent browsing moments

### Zero-Result and Low-Supply Handling
The system must define fallback behavior when local or exact matches are weak, including:
- nearby area expansion
- broader category suggestions
- similar product recommendations
- editorially boosted starter content
- prompts to create a request when supply is weak

### Professional Services Scope Clarity
For MVP, the product is optimized for farmers and buyers only. Professional service providers are intentionally out of scope as a supported user segment. A future phase may introduce a dedicated paid professional space with subscription access, structured professional profiles, service publishing, and qualified lead workflows.

### Messaging Safety and Quality
MVP messaging should include:
- block, mute, and report actions within conversations
- spam prevention rules for outbound contact
- conversation states and context persistence
- moderation response flows for abusive or suspicious behavior
- lightweight lead handling so users can understand whether a conversation is new, awaiting response, replied, archived, or blocked

### Identity and Outreach Rules
For MVP, the product should enforce simple trust rules:
- confidence-confirmed users should be visually distinguishable in profile and messaging surfaces
- low-confidence accounts may face stricter limits on repeated unsolicited outreach
- repeated spam-like contact attempts should reduce messaging ability or trigger moderation review
- when ranking quality is otherwise similar, confidence-confirmed and complete profiles should rank above low-confidence low-completeness accounts

### Launch and Liquidity Rules
For MVP launch planning, the product and operations teams should lock:
- one to three launch geographies before broad expansion
- a priority set of commercial categories with enough visible supply and demand
- weekly liquidity review by geography and category
- intervention or expansion pause rules when useful match quality declines
- explicit exclusion of professional service-provider acquisition from MVP launch goals

### First Success Interventions
MVP should include basic triggers and UX responses for:
- new listing with no views after the initial review window
- listing with views but no messages after the initial review window
- new purchase request with no replies after the initial review window
- question with no answer after the initial review window
- new user who has not reached first meaningful interaction within an early active-session window

### Minimum Quality Standards
For MVP, listings and requests should meet basic quality thresholds before they receive normal discovery distribution:
- clear title
- category
- location
- quantity when applicable
- request timing when applicable
- image encouragement or requirement where feasible for listings
- freshness state management
Items missing core fields may still be saved as drafts or published with reduced discovery until improved.

## 24. Final Recommendation

Sout El Falah should launch as a focused farmer-first web platform for Tunisia with a clear MVP centered on:
- community posts,
- agricultural listings,
- purchase requests,
- direct messaging,
- and a personalized discovery experience.

The strongest first product strategy is not to build a broad agricultural ecosystem immediately, but to build a simple, trusted, highly usable experience that continuously helps users ask, sell, discover, and connect.

If executed well, this creates the foundation for future expansion into a paid professional-services space, stronger trust systems, advanced recommendations, monetization, and a broader digital agriculture ecosystem.
